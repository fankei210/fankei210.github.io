  • Harlequin Books Sampling

    UX/UI, Strategy, Design

    Harlequin is a leading publisher of commercial fiction and narrative nonfiction in romance. The business objective of this project is to increase sales and market share of Harlequin romance novels. As the UX designer of the team, I will explore what prevents readers from reading our books, how to increase awareness of Harlequin among readers of romance fiction, and finally coming up with solutions that increase their purchase consideration.

Project Goals

  • Increase awareness, familiarity, and perceptions of Harlequin among readers of romance fiction

  • Increase purchase consideration

Research Methods

  • Persona

  • User Journey Mapping

  • User Interviews

  • Usability Testing

Discover Challenges from Readers

  • We have developed personas to understand who we are designing for with interviews and surveys. We have also discovered some of our readers are embarrassed to read our books because it seems juvenile. To them, our stories are not relevant and they are unaware of our variety of series.

Finding Opportunities with User Journey

  • With the target personas, back story, and destination in place, it's time to walk a mile in their shoes. During the exercise, we brainstormed about everything from high-level goals, pain points to opportunities and strategies.



    • Strategy 1:

      Achieve mass reach to drive awareness with insightful and target-relevant messaging
      Show up where she's already consuming entertainment/content (video, social, print)

    • Strategy 2:

      Drive sampling/purchase of Harlequin novels
      Use compelling and hard-hitting tactics to drive her to retail or sample books online to learn more about the series and make a purchase (social, display)

Wireframe & Prototype

  • After observing our users interact with the prototypes in usability tests, we can ensure that they can complete their tasks at ease with our desktop design. For the mobile version, we have discovered that our users are not clicking on the "Get Free books" buttons at the top because they were not confident to decide without reading the book descriptions. With that information, we have adjusted the design by removing the two books modules on the top completely and making sure that the book details are displayed clearly right above the buttons by mimicking the desktop experience.

Style Guide

The Result

  • The launch of the sampling campaign is well received. We have not only successfully increase awareness of Harlequin among existing readers across many channels online through Ads with multiple A/B testing, but we have also attracted many new readers that were unfamiliar with our brand to sample and try out our new books. The project is still ongoing, but this is a great start to the journey.

What We have Learned

  • Detailed qualitative research is important for successful product development. It does not only help us to raise unanswered questions but also drives us to think critically as a team to come up with more solutions. By identifying the user's persona and her needs through journey mapping, we have discovered many opportunities to make our product better in every aspect for our users.



    Another lesson we have learned from this project is that making assumptions can lead to failures. Fortunately, we were able to identify the problems early on from our prototypes with usability testing. A great product should be tailored to every device and not just one.
