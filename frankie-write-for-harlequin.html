  • Write for Harlequin

    UX/UI, Strategy, Design, Branding

    Write for Harlequin is the voice of Harlequin's romance editors. It is the place where editors share their editorial and publishing expertise and it's an invaluable resource for writers who are interested in being published by Harlequin. As an experiment approved by the stakeholders, we have decided to use the Lean UX workflow and create a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) to measure and learn along the design process.

  • The Challenge

    From the very start, our editors have provided us valuable insights into our new writers. We have then interviewed several writers to validate our hypothesis. As it turns out, our editors are very accurate about the writers' needs. The writers were having a hard time finding information on each Harlequin romance series, guides and FAQs on how to submit a story for consideration, and updates on Harlequin's author outreach initiatives.

    Another challenge of this project is to allow editors to create and post their content at ease. With buy-in from our editors, we have decided to build the site with WordPress that our editors are familiar with to make the learning curve more shallow.

Project Goals

  • Help writers to find information on each Harlequin romance series

  • Guiding writers how to submit a story for consideration

  • Provide updates on Harlequin's author outreach initiatives

  • Enrich the experience of interaction between editors and writers

Research Methods

  • Persona

  • User Journey Mapping

  • Site Mapping

  • Information Architecture

  • User Interviews

  • Usability Testing

  • Card Sorting exercise is important for this project. As we gathered feedback and learned from our MVP, we have revamped our navigation multiple times to find the balance between Breadth vs. Depth.

The Result

  • Informative Homepage

    The new homepage allows writers and editors to get the latest news and submission opportunities at ease.

  • Discover the Writers Blog

    This is the place where editors share their editorial and publishing expertise and it's an invaluable resource for writers who are interested in being published by Harlequin.

  • Submission Guide

    Allowing writers to read the submission guidelines. By using the guidelines for each series, they can determine the best fit for their story with just a couple of steps.

  • Meet the Editors behind the Scene

    Harlequin's team of over 40 editors working in London, New York and Toronto has more than 700 years of collective experience! An editor is the author's partner, collaborator and champion, committed to working with the author to produce the best book possible.

Takeaways

  • This project is one of the first to use the Lean user experience design process (Build > Measure > Learn > Repeat), and feedback from our editors and writers is essential. The product was so successful that our company decided to adapt the process to our digital projects. It showcases the value of user experience in connect people and fulfill their needs.

    Another takeaway from this project is to facilitate the workshops online. After some research, we have learned to use Trello for card sorting and Team apps to gather feedback. The tools allowed us to invite stakeholders and users online to open up many great discussions.
